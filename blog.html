The purpose of the video was a chance for Randy Potts to share the story of his gay uncle Ronnie's suicide with others. It also was an opportunity for Randy to be honest with himself and his uncle on how he really felt about his uncle Ronnie taking his life. The message of the video was if you're gay, you're not alone, there are others out there just like you with the same struggles and ways you can get help if you need support. The video achieved everything it set out to. The message accurately showed how suicide doesn't just harm you but others, and described if you are having a hard time accepting who you are there are people you can go to for help. The message is structure is very loose and personal. The message is deep and although the letter is addressed to Randy's uncle Ronnie,the whole story really applies and is relevant to all other people who may be struggling with homosexuality. The structure of this video has helped me revise my Lived Experiences Project by showing me that narratives don't really have limits. There is no right way to write a narrative, for example there is no set in stone structure of 5 paragraphs, it is a personal account and story that you get to tell in your own unique way.

South Park Analysis

The group that is targeted in the South Park episode is people who over excessively play online games, specifically World Of War Craft. The episode pokes fun at people who make video games their life. They constantly play and see the World of War Craft as important as the real world. I found the show’s creator’s view really funny on the topic. Although they are just stereotyping I think it is funny that they made an episode devoted to exposing some of the realities of online gamers. Some people really do get so involved in the online games that they lose sight of reality and what is actually important.
